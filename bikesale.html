Dashboard Explanation: Age-Based Bike Purchase Trends

This dashboard provides insights into bike sales trends based on demographic factors like marital status, education, region, and commute distance. It helps identify key patterns in purchasing behavior by visualizing data through interactive filters and charts.

Dashboards preview:

1. Filters (Left Panel)

Users can filter the data based on:

  • Marital Status: Married, Single
  • Education Level: High School, Partial College, etc.
  • Region: Europe, North America, Pacific

These filters dynamically adjust the visualizations to analyze bike sales by different customer segments.

2. Commute Distance vs. Bike Purchases (Top-Right & Bottom Charts)

Chart Type: Line Chart

Purpose: Shows how the distance a customer commutes impacts their decision to buy a bike.

Key Insights:

  • The blue line represents people who did not purchase a bike.
  • The orange line represents those who purchased a bike.
  • People with shorter commute distances (0-1 miles) have a higher count of not purchasing bikes.
  • Those commuting 5-10 miles are more likely to buy a bike, indicating that moderate commuting distances influence bike purchases positively.

3. Bike Sales Breakdown by Gender (Top-Center Chart)

Chart Type: Clustered Bar Chart

Purpose: Displays the number of bikes purchased by male and female buyers.

Key Insights:

  • The orange bars indicate the number of people who purchased a bike.
  • The blue bars indicate those who did not purchase a bike.
  • Average income values are displayed on top of each bar.
  • Males generally have a higher purchase count compared to females.

Overall Analysis

  • Demographics & Income: Higher-income individuals (both male and female) tend to purchase bikes more frequently.
  • Commute Distance Influence: Moderate-distance commuters (5-10 miles) show higher bike purchase rates.
  • Regional & Education Insights: Users can analyze specific segments by applying filters.

Key DAX Formulas Used

Total Bike Purchases

        TotalPurchases = COUNTROWS(FILTER(BikeData, BikeData[Purchased Bike] = "Yes"))

This formula counts the number of rows where a bike was purchased.

Average Income Calculation

        AvgIncome = AVERAGEX(BikeData, BikeData[Income])

This computes the average income for different customer segments.

Bike Purchase Rate by Commute Distance

        BikePurchaseRate = DIVIDE([TotalPurchases], COUNTROWS(BikeData))

This calculates the percentage of customers who bought a bike, helping us analyze trends by commute distance.
